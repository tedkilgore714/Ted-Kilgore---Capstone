# Strategy

## Track
B — personal tool (not a business/service pitch to other job seekers).

## What it does + who it's for

AI Job Scout takes your resume and target job criteria and identifies 30 companies
that are the strongest match — researched and spot-checked against growth signals,
company size, and leadership stability, not just keyword-matched against job boards.

Built for me: a Director of Professional Services job-searching in Austin, tired of
wading through stale reposts on job boards.

**Roadmap (not v1):** career-page monitoring + email alerts when new matching roles
appear at a target company. Site copy should present this as "coming next," clearly
separated from what v1 actually does.

## The ONE action

"Try the demo" — but no functional app exists yet in this repo (confirmed: only docs,
no app code). For today's build, the CTA is a **"coming soon" placeholder** (e.g.
notify-me / launching-soon), not a link to a live tool. Revisit once the demo (Phase
1-3 of the PRD's v1 scope: resume paste → 30-company recommendation → curation) is
actually built.

## Why me

I've job-hunted multiple times over my career, and the process keeps getting worse —
I've been out of work for over two years, so I have direct, current-not-stale
experience with exactly the pain this tool solves.

Concretely: I wasted hours every day applying to listings that were still posted but
no longer active, until I started manually cross-checking every LinkedIn listing
against the hiring company's own career page before investing in the full
research/tailor/apply/track cycle. That manual verification step — done by hand,
one listing at a time — is the exact workflow this tool automates and scales, and
it's time that should have gone to networking and skills growth instead.
