# Social Drafts — AI Job Scout

Short-form posts for LinkedIn/X, for the week of July 10, 2026. Grounded in what v1 actually does — no claims about career-page monitoring, digests, alerting, or Kanban tracking, since none of that is built yet.

---

## Problem 1

Every weekday, I spend 5 hours researching job openings. Not applying — researching. Scrolling job boards, opening a dozen tabs, and realizing half the listings are stale reposts that have been sitting there for weeks.

That's 25 hours a week that should go toward networking and building skills instead. The real problem was never a shortage of listings. It's finding the fresh, relevant ones from companies actually worth working for.

## Problem 2

Job boards answer "who's hiring right now" — not "is this company worth working for." A posting doesn't tell you if headcount's been growing for three years or shrinking, or whether leadership's been stable or churning through CEOs.

That's the research that actually eats the time: checking growth, size, and leadership history one company at a time. Job boards skip it entirely and leave you to do it manually, company by company.

## Solution 1

Here's what I built: AI Job Scout. Paste your resume as text, fill out a short preferences form, and Claude puts together a shortlist of 30 target companies matched to your background — each one spot-checked with live web search against headcount growth, company size, and leadership stability, sources cited.

You curate the list from there — add, remove, keep what's actually worth targeting. It's v1: no monitoring or alerts yet, just a better-researched starting shortlist than a job-board search gives you.

## Solution 2

Most job tools scrape listings and match keywords. AI Job Scout skips the listings and researches the companies instead — because the differentiator isn't finding more postings, it's watching the right companies' own career pages directly, so what surfaces later is live and real, not a repost.

V1 is the first mile: resume and preferences in, a researched 30-company shortlist out, curated by hand. Career-page monitoring and a daily digest are next, not shipped yet.

## Personal

I'm a Director of Professional Services, and I've watched 5 hours a day disappear into job-board research that should've gone toward networking and skill-building instead.

Building AI Job Scout as my capstone project was a chance to fix my own problem: instead of hunting for any listing, get a shorter, better-researched list of companies actually worth targeting. It's v1 — built for an audience of one, me, for now — but it's already changed how I approach the search.
